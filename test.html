Income and Employment in Australia

Industrial Employment in Australia

This chart illustrates employment numbers across various industries in Australia. Up until 2019, retail trade was the largest employer in the country, but healthcare overtook it, becoming Australia's top industry for employment. This change reflects the growing needs of Australia's aging population, as well as improvements in healthcare services and outcomes. The rise in healthcare jobs was further boosted by the COVID-19 pandemic, which significantly increased the demand for healthcare workers. Moreover, technological advancements in the healthcare sector have contributed to this employment growth, positioning healthcare as a key pillar of the Australian workforce.

Weekly Income per State in Australia

This choropleth map shows the median weekly income of employees in Australia. The Northern Territory had an overwhelmingly higher median in 1983, and it wasn't until 1993 that other states started to catch up with it. 2023 data shows that the Northen Territory still has the highest median weekly income in Australia, with New South Wales and Western Australia following closely behind. South Australia and Tasmania are the two states with the lowest median weekly income in Australia.

Weekly Income based on Sex and Age

This population chart illustrates that wages for Australians are lowest among those aged 15 to 19, gradually increasing until they reach their peak. For Australian females, the highest earnings occur in the age bracket of 45 to 54, while Australian males hit their peak one age group earlier, at 35 to 44. After reaching these peaks, earnings decline until retirement age, which is defined as 65 and older.

World GDP per Capita

This global choropleth map, filtered by year, highlights that Australia consistently ranks among the top countries in GDP per capita. High-income nations are generally located further from the equator, with notable exceptions like Singapore, which stands out as a regional outlier. Australia's strong GDP per capita is driven by key sectors such as mining, finance, healthcare, and education. These industries have contributed to its robust economic performance over the years, making it a leader in the Asia-Pacific region, with a stable economy that continues to attract global investments.
